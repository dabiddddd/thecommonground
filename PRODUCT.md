# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Gen Z and Millennials globally (Philippines-origin brand with international appeal) who value authentic streetwear with cultural meaning. They're style-conscious but not hype-driven — they want clothes that signal identity without shouting.

**Context:** Mobile-first shopping, social media discovery (Instagram/TikTok), mixed intent (browsing vs. purposeful purchase), price-sensitive but willing to pay for quality and story.

**Job to be Done:** "Help me express my identity through clothes that actually mean something, made by people who get it, without the markup of imported brands."

## Product Purpose

TheCommonGround is a Filipino streetwear brand proving world-class design doesn't require foreign markup. The website is the primary sales channel and brand vessel — it must communicate conviction through craft, convert browsers into believers, and make purchasing feel like joining a movement, not a transaction.

**Success looks like:** High conversion from homepage → collection → checkout, strong brand recall (return visits, social shares), low cart abandonment, organic word-of-mouth.

## Brand Personality

**Bold** — Unapologetic about origin and values. Doesn't dilute for mass appeal.
**Authentic** — No manufactured hype. Real materials, local production, honest pricing.
**Premium** — Elevated craft in every touchpoint. The site itself feels like the product: considered, weighty, worth keeping.

## Anti-references

- **Hypebeast/cluttered streetwear sites:** Aggressive neon, chaotic layouts, excessive motion, hard-to-read typography, FOMO tactics
- **Corporate/sterile minimalism:** Cold navy/gray palettes, zero personality, template-feeling SaaS landing pages, "safe" design
- **Generic DTC cream/beige aesthetic:** The saturated AI-default warm-neutral body bg, tiny uppercase eyebrows on every section, gradient text, glassmorphism cards, numbered section markers as default scaffolding

## Design Principles

1. **Conviction over conversion tricks** — No fake urgency, countdown timers, or manipulative patterns. Trust earns sales.
2. **Local pride, global standard** — Filipino-made doesn't mean "good for the Philippines." It means world-class. The site must feel at home in Tokyo, London, or New York.
3. **Weight through restraint** — Bold doesn't mean loud. Premium feels heavy because it knows what to leave out. One accent color. One display font. Generous space.
3. **Motion as craft, not decoration** — Every animation serves orientation, feedback, or delight. No gratuitous stagger. Respect reduced motion completely.
4. **Content leads, chrome follows** — Product photography and copy carry the brand. UI gets out of the way. No cards where a simple list works better.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA minimum** across all surfaces
- Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states
- Screen reader support (semantic HTML, ARIA where needed)
- `prefers-reduced-motion` honored — all animations have instant/crossfade alternatives
- Color-blind safe palette (accent tested against backgrounds)
- Touch targets ≥ 44×44px
- Language: English primary, Filipino/Tagalog considerations for local users
